About the Studio

Florida-rooted sculpture, paintings, and carved natural forms by Cassandra Plock.

Artist Statement

The Salty Vault Studio is the work of Cassandra Plock, a Florida artist building a body of work around water, wildlife, old Florida texture, and the strange little details that make natural forms feel alive.

Her current work centers on hand-carved wood sculpture, especially locally harvested Eastern Red Cedar. The carvings are shaped through traditional and power carving methods, then finished to hold the grain, movement, and character of the wood instead of hiding it. Octopus forms, wetland creatures, flowing water, suction cups, petals, shells, and tidal movement all show up as recurring visual language.

The paintings and earlier works sit in the same world: expressive, organic, water-shaped, sometimes whimsical, and often tied back to Florida ecology. The studio is not trying to imitate polished factory perfection. It is an evolving archive of handmade work, local material, conservation-adjacent observation, and the long process of becoming more deliberate without sanding away the weirdness.

The Salty Vault Studio exists as both a working studio and a growing catalog of available pieces, past experiments, and future collections. The focus is simple: make work with a clear hand, a real relationship to place, and enough movement that the piece still feels like it might crawl, bloom, swim, or shift when nobody is looking.

Available work, sculpture inventory, and archived pieces are being rebuilt into a cleaner catalog as the studio relaunches.
