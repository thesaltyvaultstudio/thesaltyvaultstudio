About the Studio

Florida-rooted sculpture and paintings by Cassandra Plock.

Artist Statement

Florida has been my home for over 30 years. Water has always shaped the rhythm of my life through family, outdoor exploration, carving, fishing, springs, coastlines, and the tangled wet ecosystems that define so much of this state.

Environmental science was a constant influence growing up through my mother’s career, and over time I began to see the same waterways that shaped my life facing increasing pressure from development and environmental neglect.

Much of my work is rooted in those experiences: kayaking through inter-coastal waterways with my husband, exploring springs and swamps with my daughter, studying wildlife movement, texture, erosion, tidal flow, and the strange organic forms that emerge where land and water constantly reshape one another.

I believe conservation and responsible progress should not be opposing ideas. My work is not meant to lecture, but to deepen connection. To remind people that these environments are alive, personal, and worth protecting.

I am drawn to carving because it feels intentional and tangible. Wood carries its own character, shaped by the water and land it grows from. The Eastern Red Cedar I use is locally harvested near the Econlockhatchee River basin. The farther the trees grow from the water source, the more “pecky” holes begin to form, destabilizing the wood. Even the material itself tells a story about water, land, pressure, and survival.

Available work, sculpture inventory, and archived pieces are being rebuilt into a cleaner catalog as the studio relaunches.
