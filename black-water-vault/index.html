Maps for seeing Florida’s wetland systems before they are treated like empty land.

The Black Water Vault is a public-facing archive for regional Florida maps showing wetlands, development, waterbodies, protected lands, and flow-accumulation patterns. The goal is not hype. The goal is visual evidence, reproducible workflow, and plain-language interpretation.

View map archive Read the method

What this is

A map archive built to explain how development pressure interacts with wetlands and likely surface-flow behavior across Florida. The maps are regional first: Water Management Districts are treated as the main hydrologic frame, with smaller Areas of Interest used later for closer inspection.

What this is not

These maps are not engineering flood models, legal wetland delineations, or parcel-level hydraulic studies. They show visible spatial relationships and hydrologic pressure patterns that can guide deeper review.

Method

Built as a reproducible GIS system, not a one-off graphic.

1. Preserve originals

Source datasets are archived intact. Processing happens only on duplicated working copies.

2. Work by region

Water Management Districts provide the regional frame so the analysis follows hydrologic governance instead of arbitrary political boundaries.

3. Separate analysis from display

Flow-accumulation and stream rasters remain analytical layers. Water masks are used only to clean visual artifacts inside lakes and flat water surfaces.

4. Document limitations

Regional maps show flow preference and hydrologic pressure. They do not claim exact flood depths or culvert-by-culvert hydraulic behavior.
